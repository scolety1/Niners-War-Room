DEFINITIONS 
 
FFL: The Fantasy Football League (FFL) comprised of FFL Teams, FFL Team Owners, FFL 
Commissioner, and a FFL Website bound by a common set of Rules agreed upon by each of 
the participants. For the purpose of this document, the FFL started at the beginning of the 
National Football League (NFL) season in the year 2002. 
 
FFL Rules: A set of standards and guidelines used to establish criteria under which the FFL 
will be run.  All FFL Team Owners must agree to the FFL Rules prior to participation in the 
FFL. 
 
FFL Commissioner: The FFL Commissioner will oversee and manage the FFL.  
 
FFL Assistant Commissioner: The person in charge of acting as FFL Commissioner in the 
absence of the FFL Commissioner.  
 
FFL Team Owner: The FFL Team Owner is the human participant in the FFL League who is 
responsible for managing an FFL Team.  
 
FFL Team: An FFL Team is a group of NFL Players assigned to a particular FFL Team 
Owner and identified by a team name.   
 
NFL Player: An NFL Player is a position player, team, or portion of a team (e.g. defense) 
which is eligible to occupy a Roster spot on an FFL Team.   
 
FFL Roster: The FFL Roster is the list of NFL Players that make up the FFL Team.  Common 
abbreviations for FFL Roster positions: 
QB – NFL Quarterback 
WR – NFL Wide Receiver 
RB – NFL Running Back 
TE – NFL Tight End 
K – NFL Place Kicker 
DEF – NFL Team Defense 
BN – FFL Bench (non-starting) Position 
IR – NFL Player designated OUT or placed on Injured Reserve by NFL Team 
 
FFL Website: The FFL Website is the official website used to manage FFL Teams, Rosters, 
Standings, Trades, and other acquisitions during the FFL Pre- season and FFL Season.  The 
FFL Commissioner will be responsible for managing the FFL Website. 
 
FFL Pre -season: The FFL Pre -season is the annual time period beginning after the FFL 
Commissioner officially declares the start of the FFL Pre- season and ending at the start of 
the first NFL game of the N FL regular season.  The FFL Pre -season will not begin until the 
FFL has successfully been set-up on the FFL Website after the annual FFL Draft.

FFL Season: The FFL Season is the annual time period beginning at the start of the first NFL 
game of the NFL regular season and ending at mid-night on the day the last NFL game of the 
NFL regular season is played.   
 
FFL Regular Season: The FFL Regular Season consists of the FFL Games from the 
beginning of the FFL Season to the beginning of the FFL Post-season.   
 
FFL Post-season (playoffs): The FFL Post-season is a qualifying tournament reserved for the 
top FFL Teams of the FFL Regular Season.   
 
FFL Offseason: The offseason is the time period from the end of the FFL Season to the 
beginning of the FFL Pre-Season. 
 
FFL Game: A head -to-head competition between two FFL Teams involving one week of 
statistics from an NFL season. 
 
FFL Standings: The FFL Standings represent the rank of each FFL Team based on 
performance during the FFL Season as compared to the other FFL Teams in the FFL.   
 
FFL Starting Roster: The NFL Players on an FFL Team which are eligible to earn points for a 
particular week of the FFL Season. 
 
FFL Bench: The NFL Player s on an FFL Team which are not eligible to earn points for a 
particular week of the FFL Season. 
 
Rights: The FFL Team Owner has the Rights to each NFL Player  assigned to their FFL 
Team.  If the NFL Player is released or Traded from the FFL Team, the FFL Team Owner 
loses Rights to the player.  
 
FFL Trade: An FFL trade is a swap of the Rights to one or more NFL Players and/or Draft 
picks owned by one FFL Team for the Rights to one or more NFL Player s and/or Draft picks 
owned by a second FFL Team.  For a Trade to become valid, both of the FFL Team Owners 
must agree to all the terms of the Trade. 
 
FFL Trading Deadline: The FFL Trading Deadline marks the time when Trading is no longer 
allowed in the FFL Regular Season. 
 
FFL Free-agent: An FFL Free-agent is an NFL Player whose Rights do not belong to any FFL 
Team.   
 
FFL Free-agent Pool: The FFL Free-Agent Pool is a list of all FFL Free-agents. 
 
Offseason Ranking Sheet: The Offseason Ranking Sheet is the official list used by the FFL to 
rank NFL Players.

Roster Declaration Day: Each FFL Team may protect the Rights of certain NFL Players on 
their FFL Team roster for the upcoming FFL Season on Roster Declaration Day.  The Roster 
Declaration Day is an annual event that will precede the annual Draft.   
 
FFL Draft: The FFL Draft is an organized event in which FFL Teams may acquire the Rights 
to FFL Free-agents in the FFL Offseason.  Each FFL Team will be assigned one Draft Pick in 
each Round of the FFL Draft.   
 
FFL Draft Round: A Round in the FFL Draft consists of the opportunity for each FFL Team to 
acquire the Rights to one FFL Free-agent. 
 
FFL Draft Pick: The FFL Draft Pick is an owned commodity which gives the opportunity of the 
FFL Team Owner to acquire the Rights to an FFL Free -agent during the FFL Draft based on 
the FFL Draft Order.  Each FFL Team will be assigned one FFL Draft Pick in each Round of 
the FFL Draft. 
 
FFL Draft Order: The Draft Order is the sequential position of each FFL Team in each Round 
of the FFL Draft. 
 
FFL Cut Day: The day Teams must  have their Rosters reduced to the allowable Roster size 
for the Regular Season.  
 
FFL Dues:   The FFL Dues are the annual assessments paid to the FFL by the FFL Team 
Owners to allow their FFL Team to be eligible for participation and Awards. 
 
FFL Fees: FFL Fees are annual assessments paid to the FFL by the Team Owners to pay for 
administration costs associated with running the FFL.  Paid Fees become the property of the 
FFL. 
 
Awards: Awards are given to FFL Team Owner s based on the performance of their FFL 
Team during the FFL Season.

RULES: 
GENERAL 
 
1. Have fun and promote an environment where all FFL participants have fun. 
2. The Commissioner of the FFL will be Andrew Trelease. 
3. The FFL Commissioner will be responsible for: 
a. Collecting and distributing information to the FFL Team Owners. 
b. Collecting FFL Dues  and Fees , and distributing Awards to the FFL 
Team Owners. 
c. Interpreting the Rules of the FFL. 
d. Serving as an arbiter  in unresolved disputes between FFL Team 
Owners. 
e. Other duties as outlined in the FFL Rules. 
4. The Assistant Commissioner of the FFL will be Brian Gillis. 
5. The FFL Assistant Commissioner will be responsible for: 
a. Performing the duties of FFL Commissioner at the request of the FFL 
Commissioner when the FFL Commissioner is unable to perform the 
required duties. 
b. Serving as an arbiter  in unresolved disputes involving the FFL 
Commissioner and other FFL Team Owners. 
c. Other duties as outlined in the FFL Rules. 
6. Yahoo! Sports Free Fantasy Football League will serve as the FFL Website. 
7. Each FFL Team Owner shall not exceed the following Roster Limits: 
• 24 Players during the FFL Offseason 
• 28 Players during the FFL Pre-Season 
• 24 Players plus an additional two (2) Players eligible to be placed in an 
IR Roster position (maximum of 26 Players total). [18] 
If a Team has more than the allowed number of NFL Players on their roster at 
the end of the FFL Season, the NFL Player(s) currently occupying the IR 
Roster spot(s) will be immediately automatically dropped into the Free- agent 
Pool s uch that maximum roster limits will be maintained during the FFL 
Offseason. [10] 
8. The FFL shall consist of 10 FFL Teams.  
9. If an FFL Team Owner chooses to leave the FFL, a new FFL Team Owner will 
be found to take over the FFL Team.  
10. The FFL Commissioner will be responsible for managing any FFL Team (s) 
which do not have associated FFL Team Owner (s) until permanent FFL Team 
Owner(s) can be found. 
a. The managing responsibilities of the FFL Commissioner shall be 
generally limited to setting Starting Rosters during the FFL Season and 
making Roster Declarations during the FFL Offseason.

OFFSEASON 
 
13. The Offseason Ranking Sheet will be obtained from a reputable third- party 
source and should represent a n FF expert’s opinion of the performance of at 
least the top 200 NFL Players for the upcoming FFL Season. 
14. The Offseason Ranking Sheet will be acquired as soon after the end of the 
FFL Season as practical and will remain as the official list for determining NFL 
Player rankings on FFL Roster Declaration Day for the entire FFL Offseason. 
15. The FFL Commissioner will be responsible for acquiring and distributing the 
Offseason Ranking Sheet to the FFL Team Owners. 
16. Every NFL Player on each FFL Team will be assigned a rank based on the 
Offseason Ranking Sheet. 
17. If any FFL Team Rosters change in the FFL Offseason  due to Trades, the 
revised rosters will be re-ranked following the change. 
18. Each FFL Team may retain the R ights of up to 19 (2025) and 23 (starting in 
2026) [18] NFL Players from the current roster at the time of the annual Roster 
Declaration Day.  In order to retain the Rights to an NFL Player for the 
upcoming FFL Season, each of the FFL Team Owners must submit the list of 
NFL Players they wish to protect to the FFL Commissioner.  The FFL 
Commissioner will review the list and  verify that none of the FFL Rules have 
been violated.  Once all of the lists have been approved by the FFL 
Commissioner, the entire list of retained and/or released NFL Players will be 
distributed to the FFL Team Owners. 
a. On the Roster Declaration Day, each FFL Team may retain the Rights 
of up to 4 of the top 5 ranked NFL Player s on their current FFL Team 
Roster. At least one of the top 5 ranked NFL Players on each FFL 
Team must be released to the FFL Free- agent Pool on Roster 
Declaration Day. 
b. All NFL Players not protected by an FFL Team by midnight on the 
Roster Declaration Day will enter the FFL Free-agent Pool.   
c. If an FFL Team does not participate in retaining the R ights of any NFL 
Players by the end of the Roster Declaration Day, the FFL 
Commissioner shall protect the top 19 (2025) and 23 (starting in 2026) 
[18] players on the FFL Team based on the Offseason Ranking Sheet.   
d. An FFL Team Owner is not required to protect any players on Roster 
Declaration Day. 
e. The Roster Declaration day should take place from one week to four 
weeks prior to the FFL Draft.

19. The annual FFL Draft will consist of 5 Rounds  starting in the 2022 FFL Draft . 
[13] 
20. The FFL Draft Order will be based on the FFL Standings from the previous 
FFL Season.  The Order will be determined as follows: 
a. The first pick of the FFL Draft will go to the FFL Team with the lowest 
rank in the FFL Standings for the FFL Regular Season in the previous 
season. 
b. The second pick of the FFL Draft will go to the FFL Team with the 
second lowest rank in the FFL Standings for the FFL Regular Season in 
the previous season. 
c. Beginning at the end of the 2010 season, the third pick of the FFL Draft 
will go to the FFL Team that wins the C onsolation Tournament in the 
FFL Post-season. [5] 
d. The tenth pick of the FFL Draft will go to the FFL Team with the highest 
rank in the FFL Standings for the FFL Post -season in the previous 
season (FFL champion). 
e. The ninth pick of the FFL Draft will go to the FFL Team with the second 
highest rank in the FFL Standings for the FFL Post -season in the 
previous season. 
f. Picks 4 [5] through 8 in the FFL Draft will be determined in ascending 
order of the ranks of the remaining FFL Teams who do not qualify under 
the provisions of a through e [5] above in the FFL Standings for the FFL 
Regular Season in the previous season.

REGULAR SEASON 
 
21. The FFL Regular Season includes week 1 through week 15 [14] of the NFL 
season. 
22. FFL Standings in the FFL Regular Season will be based on weekly head-to-
head competition between FFL Teams, in a round-robin style tournament. 
a. FFL Teams  will be ranked in descending order based on winning 
percentage.   
b. FFL Teams with equal winning percentages will be ranked in 
descending order based on the cumulative points scored up to that 
point in the FFL Season. 
23. The top 8 FFL Teams in the FFL Regular Season will be determined by the 
Standings at the end of the Regular season.   
24. The top 8 FFL Teams at the end of the FFL Regular Season will be eligible for 
the FFL Post-season.  The Teams that finish the Regular Season in positions 
1-4 will play in the c hampionship tournament in weeks 16-17 [14] of the NFL 
season. The Teams that finish the Regular Season in positions 5- 8 will play in 
the Consolation Tournament in weeks 16-17 [14] of the NFL season.[4] 
 
POST-SEASON 
 
25. The FFL Post-season includes weeks 16 through 17 [14] of the NFL season. 
26. The FFL Standings in the FFL Post-season will be based on weekly head- to-
head competition between FFL Teams in a multi-level style tournament. 
27. Playoff eligible teams will be seeded at the beginning of the FFL Post-season 
based on the Standings at the end of the FFL Regular Season. 
28. The FFL Team that wins the C hampionship Tournament in the [4]  FFL Post-
season will be considered FFL champion for the FFL Season.

TRADING 
 
29. An FFL Team Owner may not Trade an NFL Player  or FFL Draft Pick which 
he/she does not have the Rights to. 
30. There must be some perceived short-term and/or long-term value for both FFL 
Teams involved in a Trade. 
31. There is no limit to the number of NFL Player s and/or FFL Draft Picks that can 
be Traded at one time.   
32. Trades involving FFL Draft Picks: 
a. FFL Team Owners may Trade future FFL Draft Picks from  any of the 
next three annual FFL Drafts.  The FFL Commissioner will be 
responsible for keeping a record of all Traded future FFL Draft Picks. 
b. NFL Player s may be Traded for FFL Draft picks as long as Roster 
limitations are not exceeded. 
33. Trading is allowed in the FFL Pre- Season, Regular Season, and Post -season 
[14]  
34. Trades during the FFL Pre- Season, FFL Regular Season , and Post -season 
[14] will be managed under the rules established in the FFL Website: 
a. There will be a one- day appeal period for the FFL Commissioner and 
FFL Team Owners to review the proposed Trade.  During this time, FFL 
Team Owners may protest the Trade to the FFL Commissioner. 
b. If the proposed Trade violates any of the FFL Rules, the Trade may be 
rejected by the FFL Commissioner. 
c. If the proposed Trade is acceptable under the FFL Rules, the Trade will 
become effective after the appeal period.   The FFL Commissioner may 
approve the Trade prior to the end of the appeal period in certain cases. 
d. The FFL Assistant Commissioner will act in the role of FFL 
Commissioner for all trades involving the FFL Team managed by the 
FFL Commissioner.  
35. Trading is allowed during the FFL Offseason.

36. During the FFL Offseason, Trades will be processed through the FFL 
Commissioner as follows: 
a. Proposed Trades shall be sent to the FFL Commissioner for verification. 
b. The FFL Commissioner will acknowledge receipt of the proposed trade 
and send notification to the remaining FFL Team Owners.  
c. Once part b is complete, there will be a one- working day appeal period 
for other FFL Team Owners  to protest the Trade to the FFL 
Commissioner.   
d. If the proposed Trade violates any of the FFL Rules, the Trade may be 
rejected by the FFL Commissioner. 
e. If the proposed Trade is acceptable under the FFL Rules, the Trade will 
become effective after the appeal period.  The FFL Commissioner may 
approve the Trade prior to the end of the appeal period in certain cases. 
f. The FFL Assistant Commissioner will act in the role of  FFL 
Commissioner for all Trades involving the FFL Team managed by the 
FFL Commissioner.  
37. Trading Ethics:  The ethical ramifications of Trade negotiations can be a 
complex and subjective issue .  However, it is important to mention situations 
which should be avoided by FFL Team Owners while involved in Trades or 
Trade negotiations in order to maintain league harmony.  The following list is 
by no means an attempt to establish ethical “rules” for Trading, nor should a 
Trade necessarily be rejected if any of the situations occur:      
a. If you trade an injured player, you should reveal that fact to your trading 
partners. 
b. If you're asked for advice you shouldn't use the information to try to 
make your own deal without asking permission first. 
c. You should not use the details of an offer to get a better deal with 
another owner, without asking permission from the original owner. 
38. Trading will be allowed during the FFL Post-season. [14]

FREE-AGENTS 
 
39. FFL Free -agents can be picked up by FFL Team  Owners at the following 
times: 
a. During the FFL Draft at the time of the current Draft Pick which the FFL 
Team Owner has the Rights to. 
b. During the FFL Pre-Season. 
c. During the FFL Season. 
40. The FFL will include a waiver period for acquisition of Free-agents.   
Starting at the beginning of the 2010 FFL Pre-Season, the FFL will utilize the 
following waiver wire rules: [7] 
a. Starting Waiver Priority Lottery based on order of draft.  Lottery Rules: 
1. A random drawing will determine the Waiver Wire Priority for each team 
at the start of the season. 
2. The drawing will begin with highest Waiver Wire Priority position (1) and 
end with the lowest Waiver Wire Priority position (10). 
3. The number of lottery chances for each team will be determined by the 
formula: Number of Teams-Draft Order Position+1 
4. Lottery Chances for each team are exclusive of trades. [9] 
b. Time Players are placed on Waivers: 1 day. 
c. At the start of the FFL Pre-Season, players will: Follow normal Waiver 
Wire rules  
d. Waiver Priority: Continual rolling list.  Waiver claims are processed in 
waiver priority order and each successful claim moves a manager to the 
end of the waiver priority list. 
 
Starting in 2026 FFL Pre-Season, Waiver Wire claims will be based on a 
Free Agent Acquisition Budget (FAAB) system. 
In a Free Agent Acquisition Budget (FAAB) waivers system, each manager 
receives a dollar amount to place blind offers on waived players throughout 
the season. The manager with the highest offer at the end of the waiver 
period claims that player, and that offer amount is deducted from the 
team's free agent budget for the season. If multiple managers place an 
equal offer on a player, Continual rolling list tiebreak will determine the 
winner of the claim. 
• Starting FFL Pre-Season FAAB will be set at $100 per Team. 
• Starting Regular Season FAAB will be set at $100 per Team. 
• Unspent FAAB at the end of the FFL Pre-Season and Regular Season 
will not carry over. 
• FAAB may be traded for Players or Draft Picks among Teams.[20]

RULE CHANGES 
 
45. Rule Change Procedure: 
a. Rule changes may be proposed by any FFL Team Owner. 
b. A proposed Rule change must be supported by at least two Team 
Owners before becoming eligible to be considered by the FFL. 
c. Rule changes shall be submitted to the FFL Commissioner for review. 
d. The FFL Commissioner will distribute the proposed Rul e change to the 
FFL Team Owners, for discussion. 
e. If the FFL Commissioner deems that there is enough interest in the 
Rule change, it will be put up for a vote. 
f. Each Team Owner will get one vote, which shall be sent to the FFL 
Commissioner (and other FFL Team Owners if desired). 
g. The FFL Commissioner will get two votes. 
h. If the proposed change receives at least 6 votes, the Rule change will 
pass. 
i. The FFL Commissioner will determine when the Rule change takes 
effect if not part of the original vote.
